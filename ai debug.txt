## Yes — here’s the most likely culprit order, plus a minimal isolation test

If GMM works on raw images but fails after preprocessing, the usual suspects are:

### Most likely preprocessing changes
1. **Invert**
2. **Crop**
3. **Rotate with expand**
4. **Blur / median / mean / sharpen**
5. **Normalization or contrast-like scaling if it sneaks into the clustering path**
6. **Masking after transforms**

---

## Why these matter

### 1) Invert
Invert changes the intensity distribution but usually not the variance.
It’s less likely to break GMM by itself unless combined with smoothing or clipping.

### 2) Crop
Crop can create a much more uniform region than the full image, especially if the ROI is tight.

### 3) Rotate with expand
Rotation with expansion can introduce:
- interpolated pixels
- edge padding
- slightly different value distributions

That can matter a lot for covariance estimation.

### 4) Blur / median / mean / sharpen
These are the biggest suspects.

They can:
- reduce local variance
- make channels more correlated
- flatten regions into near-duplicate values

That is exactly the kind of change that makes GMM unstable.

### 5) Masking
A mask can turn a healthy image stack into a very narrow feature cloud if it selects mostly homogeneous tissue.

---

## Minimal test to isolate the bad step

Use the same 12-image stack and test GMM after each preprocessing stage.

### Test sequence
Run GMM on:

1. **raw images only**
2. **after invert**
3. **after crop**
4. **after rotate**
5. **after blur**
6. **after full preprocessing pipeline**

The first stage where GMM fails is the one to investigate.

---

## Even smaller and faster version

If you want the quickest diagnostic, test these 4 cases:

- **Case A:** raw
- **Case B:** raw + invert
- **Case C:** raw + invert + crop
- **Case D:** full pipeline

If A works and D fails, but B/C still work, then the likely cause is the filter or rotation stage.

---

## Best “binary search” approach

Instead of testing every step one by one, split the pipeline:

### Group 1
- invert
- crop
- rotate

### Group 2
- blur
- median
- mean
- sharpen
- unsharp

Test:

- raw
- group 1 only
- group 2 only
- full pipeline

If group 1 works and group 2 fails, you’ve found the problem class.
